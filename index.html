Question 1, 3 pts
Let \(f({\alpha}) = sin^{-1}{\alpha}\). Then the number of intersections of \(g(x) = x^3 + x^2 - x - 1\) and \(y = ln(\frac{3}{4}(f'({\frac{1}{2}}))^\sqrt{6\int_0^\frac{\pi}{2}\mathrm{cos}^3x\,\mathrm{d}x})\) is ___.

Question 2, 2 pts
The population, \(p\), of a city after \(t\) years, is given by \(p(t) = x^3 + 2x^2 + 5x + \frac{200}{\pi}sin({{\pi}x}) + 5000\). The rate of population growth of the city, in people per year, after 25 years, is ___.

Question 3, 1 pt
Inside a square of area 25 is inscribed a circle, which is circumscribed about a right triangle with integer lengths. The ratio of the shortest altitude of the triangle to the radius of the circle, expressed as a decimal, is ___.

Question 4, 2 pts
There exists a cylindrical building with its height perpendicular to the ground, with roof area of \(\frac{25}{\pi}ft^2\) and lateral surface area \(120ft^2\). While still in contact with the building, a snake and a sleeping ant are as far as possible from one another. Rounded to the nearest tenth, the least average speed, in feet per second, that the snake must travel, to reach the ant in 5 seconds, is ___.

Question 5, 1 pt
There are two coins; one that is unfair (it gives heads whenever flipped), and one that is fair. One of them is drawn at random and flipped 5 times. The probability that it is the unfair coin, rounded to the nearest hundredth, is ___.

Question 6, 3 pts
The volume of a sphere is \(36{\pi}cm^3\) and its rate of decrease is \(-6{\pi}cm^3/s\). The magnitude (absolute value) of the rate of decrease (in \(cm^3/s\)) of the greatest cube inscribed within the sphere is ___.

Question Stem for Questions 7 & 8
A unimodal data set, S, of 5 positive integers has a median of 4. The median, mean, and mode can be listed in an arithmetic sequence A where the median of S is the mean of A.
Question 7, 2 pts
If the sum of the entries in S is 20, the greatest possible value for the greatest number in the set is ___.
